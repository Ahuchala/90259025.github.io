The Hairy Ball Theorem

Mathematicians rarely pass up the opportunity to choose an entertaining name, and this is one of the most famous examples. If we place a continuous vector field on an $n$-dimensional sphere (a circle for $n = 1$, a hollow ball for $n = 2$, the three-sphere for $n = 3$, and so on), then whenever $n$ is even, that vector field can’t be nonzero everywhere. Put more literally, it’s impossible to comb a hairy ball without at least one hair sticking straight up.
